Analyisis

Hardin-Bernhardt Ranks is a new way of ranking NHBB teams using a modified version of the Go Elo (mElo) formula that takes into account strength of field and margin of victory.

The Formulas

Expected Score Formula

This formula uses the mElo Ratings of a given Team A and Team B to calculate what percentage of the total points Team A would be expected to score in a game against Team B. This formula will always result in a value for the Expected Score of A between 0 and 1.

\[S_E(A) = {1\over e^{D/R_A}+1}\] \[D = R_B-R_A\]

New mElo Formula

Following a game between Team A and Team B, this formula calculates the new mElo Ranking of Team A. The new mElo formula takes the percentage of the gameâ€™s points scored by Team A, multiplies it by the T-value to adjust for conversion issues, and compares it to the Expected Score for A. That result is then multiplied by the variables q, g, and K to adjust for strength of field, games played, and to increase the impact respectively.

\[R_n(A) = {R_o(A)+q({800\over g_A})[({S_A\over S_A+S_B})-S_E]}\] \[R_n = new\;rating\] \[R_o = old\;rating\] \[q = strength\;of\;field\] \[g = games\;played\] \[S = score\] \[S_E = expected\;score\]

The Variables

q-Value

The q-Value is used to represent strength of field at a tournament and intended to help improve mElo as a measure of skill unaffected by the field a team is up against, and thus more useful and accurate as a comparison between teams from different regions. It is calculated as a ratio of average total points scored per game at a given tournament : average total points scored per game on a given set, and is calculated independently for the Varsity and JV fields.

g-Value

The g-Value is a multiplier to adjust for the different formats of tournaments which lead some teams to play more or fewer games. It is set equal to 8/the number of games played by the team (not counting byes or crossover matches).

T-Value

The T-Value is used to account for the inherent issues in equating the percent chance of a given winning to the percent of total points that team will score. It is calculated as a ratio of the percent of total points scored by a given team : the percent chance given to that team to win.

K-Value

The K-Value is a multiplier set equal to 100 for the purpose of increasing the change in rankings that a game can cause.
